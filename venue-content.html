How to reach us

You can choose between arriving by car, train or airplane according to your needs and the distance you have to cover.

✈ International guests are recommended to arrive via Stuttgart Airport (IATA code STR). The airport is about 30 kilometres from the city centre of Tübingen. The journey by car takes about half an hour under normal traffic conditions. In addition, there is a direct bus line 828 (Airport Sprinter) between the airport and Tübingen city center. The journey by bus takes about one hour.

🚆 Within Germany and parts of Western Europe it is recommended to travel by train via Stuttgart. Stuttgart's main station is connected to the European high-speed rail network and is served by both ICE trains of Deutsche Bahn and French TGV. The onward journey from Stuttgart to Tübingen takes place with local train of Deutsche Bahn. The journey takes about one hour. You can check train times and fares for trains in Germany at the DB (Deutsche Bahn) websites.

🚗 Tübingen is easily accessible by car via various freeways. The four-lane Bundesstraße 27 leads directly from the Autobahn 8 at Stuttgart Messe to Tübingen. Road users on the Autobahn 81 take the Herrenberg exit coming from the north and the Rottenburg exit coming from the south. From Herrenberg the Bundesstraße 296 leads to Tübingen. From the Rottenburg exit, take the Bundesstraße 28 a as well as state roads.

Tübingen and surroudings

Tübingen is a historic university town on the banks of the river Neckar in the state of Baden-Württemberg, Germany. The town is known for its well-preserved medieval inner-city and the prestigious University of Tübingen, which was founded in 1477. Its picturesque streets, charming architecture, and numerous opportunities for cultural and recreational activities make it a popular tourist destination. Besides its historic charm, Tübingen is also a center of innovation and technology with several renowned research institutions and companies.

Tübingen will surprise you: a small, ever evolving city that remains deeply attached to its traditions.
